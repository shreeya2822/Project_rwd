Navaratri

                                             

Navratri is a major festival of Hindus . Navratri is a Sanskrit word, which means 'nine nights'. During these nine nights and ten days, nine forms of Shakti /Devi are worshipped. The tenth day is famous as Dussehra . Navratri comes four times in a year. It is celebrated from Pratipada to Navami in the months of Magh , Chaitra , Ashadh and Ashwin . The nine nights of Navratri are dedicated to three goddesses – Mahalakshmi , Mahasaraswati or Saraswati and Mahakali .Nine forms are worshiped whose names and places are respectively as follows: Nanda Devi Yogmaya (Vindhyavasini Shaktipeeth), Raktadantika (Sathur), Mata Shakumbhari Shaktipeeth (Saharanpur), Durga (Kashi), Bhima (Pinjore) and Bhramari (Bhramaramba Shaktipeeth). Navdurga says. Navratri is an important major festival which is celebrated with great enthusiasm all over India . Navratri is celebrated differently in different parts of India . This festival is celebrated on a large scale in Gujarat . Navratri celebrations in Gujarat are celebrated by playing Dandiya and Garba . This goes on all night long. The experience of Dandiya is extraordinary. Garba, a form of devotional performance in honor of the Goddess, is performed before 'Aarti' and followed by the Dandiya ceremony.

Uttarayan

                                             

Uttarayan is a Hindu festival in celebration of the winter solstice and the transition to springtime, as well as the harvest. The name Uttarayan often refers to the period between the winter solstice and the summer solstice, but sometimes the name is used to refer to a celebration lasting a few days.It is celebrated annually on January 14 else on January 15 in case of a leap year. History: According to the Hindu epic Mahabharata, it was noted that King Bhishma was given the boon of choosing his own time and day of death and Bhishma Pitamah had waited for the sun to be in Uttarayana to embrace death.

Shamlaji Fair

                                             

Shamlaji fair is an annual festival held in Gujarat, India. It is held on the day of Karthiki Purnima. The fair is attended by a large number of tribals, who have great faith in Shamlaji (Baliya Bawji). Shamlaji, also spelled Shamalaji, is a major Hindu pilgrimage centre in Aravalli district of Gujarat state of India. The Shamlaji temple is dedicated to Vishnu. Several other Hindu temples are located nearby.

Vautha fair is one of the important fairs held in Gujarat. Vautha is located on the border of Dholka taluka of Ahmedabad district and Matar taluka of Kheda district. Vautha fair is the largest fair in Ahmedabad district.The importance of this place in the center of Gujarat is in the Puranas.
